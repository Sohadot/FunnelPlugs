Plug Ontology v1.0 — Class 03

Decision Clarity Failure

The path asks for a decision before it has provided the structural conditions for one to be made. The cognitive cost of deciding exceeds the buyer's readiness to act.

Class overview

Decision Clarity Failure is the structural condition in which the commercial path presents an offer, option, or action request that the buyer cannot evaluate with sufficient confidence. The path generates evaluation engagement but cannot convert it to commitment because the decision architecture is structurally insufficient.

Formal definition

The commercial path presents an offer, option, or action request that the buyer cannot evaluate with sufficient confidence to make a committed decision. The cognitive cost of deciding exceeds the buyer's readiness to act, producing hesitation, stalling, or exit. The path is asking for a decision before it has provided the structural conditions for one to be made.

Structural meaning

Decision Clarity Failure is distinct from Trust Infrastructure Failure. A buyer experiencing trust failure may understand the offer but not feel safe proceeding. A buyer experiencing decision clarity failure may feel broadly safe but cannot parse what they are being asked to agree to, which option to choose, or what the terms of the decision are. The failure is in the clarity architecture of the offer presentation, not in the trust architecture. Rewriting the headline does not resolve it. Restructuring how the offer is presented and how the decision is framed does.

Where it appears

At offer presentation stages — pricing pages, plan comparison views, multi-option checkout flows, and any surface where the buyer must choose between alternatives or assess the terms of a single offer. Also present in content-heavy paths where information volume obscures the decision rather than clarifying it. Present in high-option environments where differentiation between choices is insufficiently explained.

What it damages

Decision momentum, offer comprehension, and the buyer's ability to move from evaluation to commitment. Extended evaluation time without conversion is a diagnostic signal for this class. When buyers understand the product but cannot evaluate the specific offer, they remain in evaluation mode indefinitely — producing high time-on-site metrics alongside low conversion rates.

Typical symptoms

Extended session time on pricing or offer pages followed by high exit rates with no action taken. Support queries requesting clarification on offer terms, pricing logic, or feature differences that the page presents but does not adequately explain. Multiple page revisits before abandonment. Buyers who engage across multiple sessions without completing a decision. Pricing page exits to competitor comparison sites.

Diagnostic logic

Map the decision-critical surface in the path. Assess whether the information hierarchy makes the most decision-relevant facts immediately visible and interpretable. Identify whether multiple options are differentiated clearly enough to support a choice. Check whether the terms of the commitment — what the buyer is agreeing to — are stated with sufficient specificity. If the page requires significant cognitive work to identify the right option or understand the offer terms, Decision Clarity Failure is the active structural condition.

Required plug logic

Offer architecture review to reduce cognitive friction at decision stages. Clear information hierarchy: the most decision-critical facts must be immediately visible and interpretable without requiring active search. Explicit differentiation where multiple paths exist, with the differentiating criteria made structural rather than left to the buyer to infer. Removal of decision-irrelevant complexity from conversion surfaces. The intervention is architectural: not more content but better-organised content at the decision stage.

Intervention class: Decision Guidance Layer

Relationship to the Funnel Integrity Standard

The Funnel Integrity Standard requires that the path sustain confidence at decision-critical points. Decision Clarity Failure violates the Trust Integrity dimension because insufficient clarity prevents the confidence required for commitment. A buyer who cannot evaluate what they are committing to cannot make a confident decision regardless of their level of product trust.

Primary standard dimension: Trust Integrity

Relationship to the FunnelPlugs Protocol

In the FunnelPlugs Protocol, Decision Clarity Failure is assigned during Classification. Following Detection — where evaluation engagement without conversion is identified at offer stages — Classification distinguishes this class from Trust Infrastructure Failure based on the pattern: extended evaluation time and clarity-seeking behaviour rather than commitment-stage trust failure.

Protocol stage: Classification

Relationship to the Revenue Leak Diagnostic Engine

Decision Clarity Failure maps to the Decision Ambiguity leak class in the Revenue Leak Diagnostic Engine's diagnostic taxonomy. Declared signals of extended evaluation time, option confusion, plan comparison difficulty, and clarity-related support contact route engine output toward this class.

Relationship to the Plug Registry

The Plug Registry's Decision Guidance Layer is the corresponding intervention category. Registry entries in this category define the structural mechanisms for reducing cognitive friction at decision stages: information hierarchy, option differentiation, and commitment-term clarity.

Representative diagnostic scenario

Representative diagnostic scenario — not client data.

A commercial SaaS path presents three pricing tiers. Each tier lists a set of features. The differentiating features between tiers are described using internal product terminology that buyers who have not yet used the product cannot interpret. The most important differentiating factor — the usage limit that determines which tier is appropriate for a given buyer's scale — is present on the page but not visually prominent. Buyers who reach the pricing page engage for an above-average session duration. Most exit without selecting a tier. A small percentage contacts support to ask which tier they need. The structural failure is not in the offer itself but in the architecture of how the decision between tiers is presented to buyers who lack the internal context needed to interpret the differentiation as presented.

Claim boundary

This class identifies a structural failure condition. It does not estimate revenue recovery, predict conversion lift, or guarantee performance improvement. Commercial impact is described qualitatively. Representative scenarios are illustrative and are not drawn from client engagements. No claim on this page constitutes a performance promise.
