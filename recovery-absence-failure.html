Plug Ontology v1.0 — Class 06

Recovery Absence Failure

The path treats every exit as final. When abandonment occurs, the system has no governed response. A recoverable portion of commercial value is lost because the path was never designed to reclaim it.

Class overview

Recovery Absence Failure is the structural condition in which the commercial path has no mechanism for re-engaging buyers who abandoned before completing a conversion. The failure is not visible within the active path — it is defined by what the path does not do after exit.

Formal definition

The commercial path lacks the structural mechanisms required to re-engage buyers who exited before conversion. When abandonment occurs, the system has no governed response. The path treats every exit as final, eliminating a recoverable portion of commercial value that the active path could not capture but a recovery system could.

Structural meaning

Recovery Absence Failure is structural because it is not corrected by improving the active path. The active path may be well-constructed and may convert the buyers it retains at a commercially viable rate. The structural gap is in what happens after those it does not retain have exited. Every commercial path loses some buyers before conversion. The question is whether the system is designed to give those buyers a second structural opportunity to complete. A path without a recovery layer accepts a lower ceiling on total conversion without attempting to reclaim what it could.

Where it appears

As a post-path structural layer. The absence is not visible within the active funnel but becomes decisive when abandonment events accumulate. Applies to cart abandonment, form abandonment, lead drop-off, trial expiry without conversion, and any non-completion event where re-engagement within a defined window is commercially viable. The failure class applies specifically to commercial paths that have sufficient abandonment volume to make recovery viable but no recovery infrastructure to act on it.

What it damages

Revenue recovery rate, total path yield, and the efficiency of the entire acquisition investment. Recovery Absence Failure means the commercial system accepts a lower ceiling on conversion without attempting to reclaim the portion the active path could not close. Every unrecovered abandonment event represents compounded cost: the acquisition cost that brought the buyer to the path, the engagement cost spent moving them through it, and the lost conversion value when they exit without completing.

Typical symptoms

No re-engagement sequence following cart or form abandonment events. No retargeting logic tied to specific abandonment stages. Customer records showing high evaluation engagement followed by permanent, unaddressed exit. No differentiated recovery path for users who reached different stages before abandonment — if recovery logic exists at all, it addresses all abandonment events with a single generic sequence rather than stage-specific re-engagement.

Diagnostic logic

Identify every abandonment event in the commercial path that occurs at a stage where re-engagement within a defined window is commercially viable. For each such event, determine whether a governed recovery sequence exists. Assess whether the recovery sequence is tied to the specific stage of abandonment or is a generic re-engagement. Measure the proportion of abandonment events that receive no recovery response. If that proportion is high and abandonment volume is material, Recovery Absence Failure is the active structural condition.

Required plug logic

Abandonment detection at each stage where recovery logic is commercially viable. Sequenced re-engagement designed to address the specific stage of abandonment rather than generic follow-up divorced from context. Timing logic calibrated to buyer decision cycles, not arbitrary intervals. Recovery paths that re-enter the commercial journey at the correct abandonment stage, not at the beginning. The intervention is not a single abandonment email — it is a governed recovery architecture across all material abandonment events.

Intervention class: Recovery Systems Layer

Relationship to the Funnel Integrity Standard

Recovery Readiness is the Funnel Integrity Standard dimension that Recovery Absence Failure directly tests against. The standard requires that the commercial system include mechanisms for restoring momentum after abandonment or hesitation. A path without recovery infrastructure fails this dimension regardless of how well it performs on trust and flow dimensions.

Primary standard dimension: Recovery Readiness

Relationship to the FunnelPlugs Protocol

The FunnelPlugs Protocol identifies Recovery Absence Failure during the Detection stage, where path mapping reveals the absence of post-abandonment re-engagement infrastructure. Unlike other failure classes that are detected through observable symptoms within the active path, this class is detected through what is structurally absent rather than what is structurally present and failing.

Protocol stage: Detection

Relationship to the Revenue Leak Diagnostic Engine

Recovery Absence Failure maps directly to the Recovery Absence leak class in the Revenue Leak Diagnostic Engine's diagnostic taxonomy. Declared absence of recovery sequences, abandonment-stage data, and re-engagement infrastructure route diagnostic output toward this class. The engine flags this class as a structural prerequisite: other improvements to the active path yield diminishing returns while recoverable abandonment goes unaddressed.

Relationship to the Plug Registry

The Plug Registry's Recovery Systems category is the corresponding intervention class. Registry entries define the structural mechanisms required to build and operate a governed recovery architecture: abandonment detection, stage-specific re-engagement sequencing, timing logic, and recovery path design.

Representative diagnostic scenario

Representative diagnostic scenario — not client data.

A lead generation path for a professional services firm receives a material volume of form starts that do not complete. The form is multi-field and requires professional information. Buyers who begin the form and do not complete it receive no subsequent communication. The firm's CRM contains records of these partial completions — the buyer's email address is captured at step one before the drop-off occurs. No automated or manual re-engagement sequence uses these records. The commercial path has the data required to operate a recovery sequence but no structural mechanism to act on it. Buyers who were commercially interested enough to begin the form, and who were reachable at the contact information they provided, receive no recovery opportunity. The structural failure is not in the form or the offer — it is in the absence of the recovery layer that should follow every partial form completion.

Claim boundary

This class identifies a structural failure condition. It does not estimate revenue recovery, predict conversion lift, or guarantee performance improvement. Commercial impact is described qualitatively. Representative scenarios are illustrative and are not drawn from client engagements. No claim on this page constitutes a performance promise.
