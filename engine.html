Governed Instrument FP-ENGINE-RLDE-V1

Revenue Leak Diagnostic Engine

A governed diagnostic instrument for identifying structural revenue leakage across digital funnels and determining the intervention logic required to restore commercial path integrity.

Engine role

The Revenue Leak Diagnostic Engine maps declared funnel conditions against the Plug Ontology. It does not estimate revenue recovery, predict conversion lift, or replace expert audit. It classifies structural failure signals into governed failure classes so the operator can identify where the commercial path appears structurally incomplete.

Output is a governed diagnostic classification — not a revenue prediction.

Purpose

Structural Diagnosis

To classify structural leakage, score funnel integrity dimensions, and recommend the appropriate plug classes without resorting to decorative metrics or unsupported financial claims.

Method

Rule-Based / Auditable

This instrument is a structural diagnostic model. It does not produce financial forecasts, market benchmarks, or unsupported performance claims. It evaluates declared funnel conditions through governed scoring and decision logic.

Time

4 Minutes

20 governed questions across 5 diagnostic modules.

Method Boundaries

What This Instrument Does — And Does Not Do

It Does

  • Classify structural leakage

    Identify the dominant and secondary leak classes through governed scoring and explicit resolution rules.

  • Score integrity dimensions

    Measure trust, continuity, clarity, friction containment, and recovery readiness in a reviewable form.

  • Recommend intervention classes

    Map diagnosed structural weakness to governed plug classes without collapsing the result into shallow product recommendations.

It Does Not

  • Estimate revenue recovery

    The instrument does not produce financial projections, revenue recovery estimates, or monetised impact claims of any kind.

  • Predict conversion lift

    No conversion rate improvement is predicted, implied, or modelled. The output is a structural classification, not a performance forecast.

  • Replace expert audit

    The instrument evaluates declared conditions, not observed behavioural telemetry. Expert review of actual user behaviour remains a separate and necessary process.

Diagnostic Sequence

Declare Funnel Conditions

Answer each question based on the current state of your funnel. Responses are used to classify structural failure and score integrity dimensions. No data is stored or transmitted.

Module 1 Message and Path Alignment

How clearly does the primary entry message describe what the visitor will find, do, or receive on the page?

1 — Not at all clear
5 — Completely clear

How well does the page content match the promise or expectation created by the entry point (ad, email, search result, or referral)?

1 — Complete mismatch
5 — Perfect match

How well does the page structure guide visitors toward the intended action without requiring them to reorient or search?

1 — No guidance at all
5 — Clear and guided

How consistent is the tone, language, and visual style between the entry point and the landing page?

1 — Completely inconsistent
5 — Fully consistent
Module 2 Trust and Confidence Architecture

How well does the page establish credibility before asking for commitment (purchase, signup, contact)?

1 — No credibility signals
5 — Strong credibility

How well does the page use social proof, testimonials, case studies, or external validation?

1 — No proof present
5 — Strong proof architecture

How well does the page address risk, concern, or hesitation at the point where commitment is requested?

1 — No risk handling
5 — Risk is fully handled

How prominent are trust-reducing elements on the page (e.g. broken links, outdated content, inconsistent design, missing security signals)?

1 — Severe trust erosion
5 — No trust erosion
Module 3 Flow and Continuity

How smoothly does the visitor move from entry to the first meaningful action without being asked to reorient?

1 — Highly disruptive
5 — Completely smooth

How many unnecessary steps, clicks, or decisions does the visitor encounter before reaching the conversion point?

1 — Excessive steps
5 — No unnecessary steps

How much cognitive effort is required to complete the form, checkout, or registration process?

1 — Overwhelming effort
5 — Minimal effort required

How often does the page introduce distractions, competing calls to action, or off-path links that draw visitors away from the primary conversion goal?

1 — Constant distractions
5 — No distractions
Module 4 Decision Clarity and Offer Interpretation

How clearly does the page communicate what the visitor is committing to (the exact offer, outcome, or next step)?

1 — Completely unclear
5 — Completely clear

How well does the page explain the value of taking the action relative to the effort or cost involved?

1 — Value is not explained
5 — Value is well explained

How well does the primary call to action communicate what happens next — not just what to click?

1 — No indication of what follows
5 — Next steps are clear

How much ambiguity exists about the price, terms, or commitment level involved in taking the action?

1 — Severe ambiguity
5 — No ambiguity
Module 5 Recovery and Re-entry

How well does the system capture and re-engage visitors who leave before converting?

1 — No re-engagement system
5 — Strong re-engagement system

How well does the follow-up sequence (email, retargeting, or other) maintain continuity with the original funnel promise?

1 — No follow-up continuity
5 — Full follow-up continuity

How well does the system offer a meaningful alternative path for visitors who are not yet ready to convert?

1 — No alternative offered
5 — Strong alternative path

How well does the system track and act on signals of intent across multiple sessions or touchpoints?

1 — No cross-session tracking
5 — Full cross-session intelligence

All responses are processed locally. No data is stored or transmitted. The report is generated on your device.
