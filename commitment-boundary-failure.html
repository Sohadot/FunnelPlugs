Plug Ontology v1.0 — Class 05

Commitment Boundary Failure

The path closes the commercial case without closing the commitment case. At the final action stage, the buyer understands the offer but encounters unresolved structural ambiguity about what the act of committing means.

Class overview

Commitment Boundary Failure is the structural condition in which the final conversion action carries unresolved ambiguity about its scope, reversibility, or immediate consequences. The buyer has evaluated the offer and found it acceptable but cannot execute the commitment without clarity that the path has not provided.

Formal definition

The commercial path does not adequately define or reduce the perceived risk, reversibility, or commitment magnitude of the final conversion action. The buyer reaches the commitment moment unable to accurately assess what they are agreeing to, or insufficiently prepared for the level of commitment being requested. The path closes the commercial case without closing the commitment case.

Structural meaning

Commitment Boundary Failure is distinct from both Trust Infrastructure Failure and Decision Clarity Failure. Trust Infrastructure Failure is about the absence of confidence architecture across the path. Decision Clarity Failure is about the difficulty of evaluating the offer. Commitment Boundary Failure occurs specifically at the execution moment — when the buyer must take the final action — and is characterised by unresolved questions about what that action means: what am I agreeing to, what happens next, can I reverse this. These questions, if unanswered by the path at the point of action, constitute a structural barrier.

Where it appears

At the final stage of the conversion path: order confirmation, subscription initiation, contract submission, and account creation with payment. This failure class is specific to the execution moment and does not appear at earlier evaluation stages. The structural gap is in the commitment framing layer immediately adjacent to the action element, not in the broader offer presentation.

What it damages

Final-step conversion rate and the psychological readiness of the buyer to execute. Also damages post-conversion satisfaction and refund or cancellation rates: buyers who proceed without commitment boundary clarity often reverse the commitment shortly after, because the ambiguity they experienced at the moment of action was not resolved by the act of committing — it was merely deferred.

Typical symptoms

Last-step abandonment despite high engagement throughout the rest of the path. Elevated refund or cancellation rates within a short post-conversion window, particularly when buyers report confusion about terms. Buyers who complete a form but do not submit. Support queries immediately following conversion asking for clarification on terms, cancellation policy, or what happens next. Post-conversion disputes that correlate with unclear commitment framing at the point of action.

Diagnostic logic

Identify the final action stage. Assess whether the commitment scope is explicitly stated: what the buyer is agreeing to, what the immediate post-commitment sequence is, and what options exist after commitment. Identify whether reversibility information, cancellation terms, or risk reduction signals are positioned immediately adjacent to the action element or are buried in secondary locations requiring active search. Measure last-step abandonment rate against earlier-step rates: disproportionate final-step exits suggest this class.

Required plug logic

Explicit commitment framing at the final action stage: what the buyer is agreeing to, what happens immediately after conversion, and what options exist post-commitment. Risk reduction signals positioned immediately adjacent to the commitment action element. Clear terms presentation without burying commitment-relevant information in secondary locations. The intervention is not a redesign of the offer but a structural addition of commitment boundary clarity at the specific point where the action is taken.

Intervention class: Friction Reduction Layer

Relationship to the Funnel Integrity Standard

The Funnel Integrity Standard's Trust Integrity dimension requires that confidence be sustained at decision-critical points. Commitment Boundary Failure produces a specific trust deficit at the final action stage: not distrust in the brand or product, but structural uncertainty about what the commitment entails. This uncertainty violates the Trust Integrity condition at the moment it is most consequential.

Primary standard dimension: Trust Integrity

Relationship to the FunnelPlugs Protocol

In the FunnelPlugs Protocol, Commitment Boundary Failure is identified during Classification based on its final-stage location and symptom pattern. The Intervention stage addresses it with commitment-boundary-specific structural additions rather than with broad trust architecture changes, because the failure is localised to the execution moment.

Protocol stage: Classification and Intervention

Relationship to the Revenue Leak Diagnostic Engine

Commitment Boundary Failure contributes to the Friction Overload leak class in the Revenue Leak Diagnostic Engine's diagnostic taxonomy, specifically at the commitment execution stage. Declared final-step abandonment signals, post-conversion reversal patterns, and buyer confusion indicators at the action stage route diagnostic output toward this class.

Relationship to the Plug Registry

The Plug Registry's Friction Reduction Layer addresses the commitment execution specific subset of friction. Registry entries in this category define mechanisms for reducing the structural friction of executing a commitment: commitment scope clarity, reversibility information, and risk reduction at the point of action.

Representative diagnostic scenario

Representative diagnostic scenario — not client data.

A subscription service path presents an offer clearly and builds adequate trust through the evaluation stages. At the final payment step, the buyer encounters a subscription initiation form. The form contains a submit button labelled 'Complete order.' The page does not state the billing frequency, the first billing date, or the cancellation process. The buyer proceeds. Within seven days, a portion of these buyers contacts support to cancel, citing surprise at the billing date or a misunderstanding of the cancellation terms. A separate portion of buyers who reached the same payment step did not complete the form despite showing high evaluation engagement. The structural failure is not in the offer evaluation path — it is in the absence of commitment boundary clarity at the specific moment of execution. The buyer needed to know what completing the order meant before they could execute it with confidence.

Claim boundary

This class identifies a structural failure condition. It does not estimate revenue recovery, predict conversion lift, or guarantee performance improvement. Commercial impact is described qualitatively. Representative scenarios are illustrative and are not drawn from client engagements. No claim on this page constitutes a performance promise.
