ARCHITECTURE.md

1) Purpose

This document defines the structural architecture of Funnelplugs.com.

Its role is to translate the Foundation Doctrine into a disciplined build system. It governs:

page architecture,

content hierarchy,

folder structure,

generation flow,

internal linking logic,

and controlled future expansion.


Funnelplugs must not be built as a loose collection of pages. It must be built as a coherent strategic asset with a clear structural spine.


---

2) Architectural Principle

The architecture of Funnelplugs must reflect its role as a reference system for funnel integrity.

That means:

structure must be intentional,

hierarchy must be clear,

every page must have a defined function,

utility must emerge from doctrine,

and expansion must remain governed.


Architecture is not layout alone. It is the organizational expression of the asset’s conceptual authority.


---

3) First-Release Structural Spine

The first release must remain controlled and sovereign.

The structural spine of the asset consists of five primary layers:

3.1 Manifesto

The founding declaration of the asset. This page explains why Funnelplugs exists, what it sees that the market misses, and why the concept matters.

3.2 Protocol

The methodological system. This page explains how Funnelplugs reads leakage, intervention classes, structural incompleteness, and funnel integrity.

3.3 Funnel Integrity Standard

The formal standards layer. This page defines what a structurally sound digital commercial journey requires.

3.4 Plug Registry

The governed reference layer. This page or section organizes plugs, intervention classes, or approved strategic response categories under a controlled structure.

3.5 Revenue Leak Engine

The utility layer. This page translates the theory into practical evaluation, diagnosis, or guided decision support.

These five layers form the first architectural spine. Everything else must support or extend them.


---

4) Page Hierarchy Logic

The hierarchy must follow authority order.

Tier 1 — Foundational Pages

These define the asset. Examples:

Home

Manifesto

Protocol

Funnel Integrity Standard

Plug Registry

Revenue Leak Engine

About / Thesis equivalent if strategically justified


Tier 2 — Structural Support Pages

These clarify or deepen the system. Examples:

methodology pages

explanatory standards subpages

category pages within the registry

report overview pages


Tier 3 — Controlled Expansion Pages

These scale the asset without diluting it. Examples:

high-value articles

deep reference pages

use-case pages

benchmark pages

selected comparative analyses


No page may be created outside this hierarchy without explicit structural justification.


---

5) Internal Linking Architecture

Internal linking must reinforce structure, not imitate generic SEO blogs.

Required behavior

Foundational pages must link clearly to one another.

Registry pages must connect upward to the Standard and Protocol.

Utility pages must connect back to doctrine and interpretation layers.

Supporting pages must strengthen core authority pages.

No important page should be orphaned.


Forbidden behavior

random internal link stuffing,

artificial density for SEO manipulation,

disconnected clusters,

loops that confuse hierarchy.


The asset must always feel navigationally governed.

8) Build and Generation Architecture

Funnelplugs follows a GitHub-first sovereign build chain.

Operational sequence

1. content/data definition


2. local Python generation/update


3. local review


4. GitHub Desktop commit/push


5. GitHub repository update


6. GitHub Actions validation/build/deployment


7. production publication



Core scripts

build.py — orchestrates the full build process

generate_pages.py — generates controlled pages from templates and data

generate_sitemap.py — generates the sitemap according to sitemap policy

validate_content.py — checks metadata, structure, slug integrity, and page validity

quality_gate.py — blocks deployment when standards are not met

config.py — centralizes settings, domain, and structural constants


The architecture must always privilege determinism over improvisation.

---

11) Expansion Doctrine

Expansion is allowed only when it strengthens the asset’s authority position.

Acceptable expansion

new standard pages

new registry categories

controlled glossary growth

selected reports

high-value comparative pages

reference-grade explanatory pages


Unacceptable expansion

thin content sprawl

topic drift

opportunistic trend posting

article volume for its own sake

pages that do not connect back to the sovereign spine


Expansion must remain architectural, not accidental.


---

12) Automation Relationship

Automation must strengthen control, not create disorder.

Automation is allowed for:

page generation,

sitemap generation,

metadata validation,

quality checks,

and deployment workflow.


Automation is not allowed to:

mass-produce weak pages,

bypass review logic,

generate uncontrolled public sprawl,

or dilute editorial discipline.


Automation serves sovereignty. It does not replace judgment.
