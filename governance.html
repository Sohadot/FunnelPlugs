Governance layer

How Funnelplugs Is Governed

Funnelplugs is a governed reference system. Its terminology is stable by design, its claims are restrained by policy, and its structural definitions change only through a documented process.

Why a reference system requires governance

A reference system that changes its definitions without notice is not a reference system. It is a publishing platform. The difference matters because practitioners who rely on a classification framework — using its terms, building workflows around its categories, citing its definitions — need those definitions to remain stable. Silent rewrites break that dependency without warning.

Governance is the mechanism that separates a category artifact from a content resource. It defines who can change what, how changes are documented, what cannot be changed at all, and how the historical record is preserved. This page describes how that mechanism works for Funnelplugs.

Why structural funnel failure needs stable terminology

Structural funnel failure is not a vague concept. It has specific patterns: friction overload, trust collapse, flow breaks, decision ambiguity, recovery absence. Each pattern has a defined type, a defined set of signals, and a defined class of intervention that addresses it.

For this classification to be useful across practitioners, the names and definitions of these types must remain consistent. A practitioner who diagnoses "trust collapse" in a client's funnel today must be working from the same structural definition as a practitioner who encountered the same diagnosis a year ago. If the term has been silently redefined in the interval, the diagnosis may look the same but describe a different condition.

Stable terminology is not a bureaucratic preference. It is a functional requirement of a reference system that aims to produce consistent diagnostic results.

How the reference system changes

Changes to Funnelplugs follow a defined process. The process has six stages: proposal, review, validation, decision log, publication, and post-publication verification.

Changes are classified by scope. Minor changes — precision improvements, new entries within existing categories, metadata corrections — require only validation and publication. Major changes — alterations to classification structure, scoring rules, protocol stages, or category hierarchy — require a written proposal, a review against existing structure, a decision log entry, and validation before publication. Doctrine-level changes — changes to the governing thesis, the ontology's foundational structure, or the formal integrity standard — require all stages and are treated as version increments.

Not all changes can be made. Established ontology terms cannot be silently modified. Published standard or protocol versions cannot be retroactively edited. Registry entries cannot be deleted. The system preserves its historical record because that record is part of its value to practitioners.

How claims are restrained

Funnelplugs makes structural and diagnostic claims. It does not make revenue, ROI, or conversion-lift claims.

The distinction is precise. Structural claims describe the architecture of commercial path failure: what type of failure exists, what pattern it follows, what class of intervention addresses it. These claims can be evaluated against the structure of a specific funnel without reference to revenue outcomes. They are either accurate or inaccurate based on whether the structural condition they describe is present.

Revenue claims are different. Whether applying a given intervention produces a specific revenue improvement depends on the specific system being corrected, the quality of execution, the market conditions, and many other factors outside the scope of structural classification. A reference system that attributes specific revenue lift to intervention classes is making claims it cannot support from structural evidence alone.

The governance policy prohibits specific revenue percentage improvements, guaranteed conversion lift claims, ROI projections not derived from the user's own data, and comparison claims against competitor tools or approaches. These prohibitions are unconditional.

How trust is protected

Trust in a reference system is built through consistency and destroyed through drift. A practitioner who returns to Funnelplugs after six months must find the same structural definitions they relied on when they first used the system, or an explicitly documented and versioned update to those definitions with a rationale they can evaluate.

The append-only policy governs the historical record: once a term is published, it is preserved. Deprecated terms are retained with a deprecation notice and a pointer to their successor. No entry disappears silently. The decision log records why changes were made and what they replaced. The version policy assigns version numbers to each layer so the scope of any change is legible to anyone reading the record.

The quality gate enforces that no page is published with structural errors, missing metadata, broken internal links, or inline scripts that the governance policy has not authorized. These are not aspirational standards — they are blocking conditions enforced by the CI pipeline before any change reaches the live system.

How governance supports category authority

Category authority in a reference domain is established by being the system that defines how the domain is understood — not the most trafficked, not the most updated, but the most structurally correct and stable. Practitioners adopt the vocabulary of the reference they trust most. That trust depends on consistency over time.

Governance is what makes consistency over time possible at scale. Without a defined change process, a defined versioning policy, a defined append-only record, and a defined claims restraint policy, the system would drift — gradually becoming less precise, less internally consistent, and less trustworthy as the population of contributors and editorial decisions grows.

Governance is therefore not overhead on top of the content work. It is the mechanism that makes the content work durable and the category position defensible.
