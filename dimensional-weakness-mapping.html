Dimensional weakness mapping

Dimensions reveal where the path fails even when funnels appear complete. Mapping weakness across all three simultaneously is the only way to see the compound effect.

Scope

This concept defines the cross-dimensional diagnostic approach for evaluating structural integrity across the three dimensions of the Funnel Integrity Standard. Dimensional weakness mapping governs how multi-class failures are identified and sequenced for intervention under the Protocol.

What dimensional weakness mapping is

Dimensional weakness mapping is the diagnostic process of evaluating a commercial path's structural integrity across all three dimensions of the Funnel Integrity Standard simultaneously — Trust Integrity, Flow Continuity, and Recovery Readiness — rather than assessing each dimension in isolation. The distinction is critical: isolated dimension audits produce partial pictures. Cross-dimensional mapping produces the compound picture that governs intervention sequencing and resource allocation.

The Funnel Integrity Standard defines three orthogonal dimensions because structural failure in a commercial path can originate in any of them, and because failure in one dimension has measurable consequences for the others. A path may exhibit strong Flow Continuity — buyers move through steps without technical obstruction — while simultaneously failing on Trust Integrity at the offer threshold. The flow-level data looks healthy; the conversion data does not. Without dimensional mapping, the diagnostic conclusion is wrong: the team optimizes flow when the problem is trust.

Dimensional weakness mapping makes the full picture visible. It answers not just "where does the path fail?" but "which dimensions are compromised, how do those compromises interact, and what is the correct intervention sequence?" The process draws on diagnostic signal layers — entry signals, flow signals, and exit signals — to populate each dimension's weakness profile before constructing the cross-dimensional analysis.

The output of dimensional weakness mapping is not a list of problems. It is a governed structural assessment with interaction effects identified, severity weighting applied, and intervention sequencing recommended. This is the input to the plug prioritization decision and the entry point for the Revenue Leak Diagnostic Engine.

The three-dimension framework

Trust Integrity dimension governs the degree to which the commercial path establishes, maintains, and operationalizes buyer confidence throughout the journey. Trust Integrity weakness manifests as a gap between stated offer value and the buyer's belief in that value — a structural absence of trust infrastructure. In isolation, Trust Integrity weakness looks like a conversion problem: visitors engage but do not purchase. The underlying cause is not the offer itself but the absence of the mechanisms that make the offer credible. Indicators include social proof deficit, authority signal absence, risk reversal weakness, and commitment-escalation failure. Trust Integrity is the foundational dimension: without it, even structurally complete Flow and Recovery cannot produce sustainable conversion rates.

Flow Continuity dimension governs the degree to which the commercial path removes structural, cognitive, and mechanical obstacles to forward progression. Flow Continuity weakness manifests as path interruption — steps that produce dropout because they require disproportionate effort, create confusion, or create friction that was not present at entry. In isolation, Flow Continuity weakness looks like a UX or checkout problem. The structural cause is the absence of friction reduction architecture at the points where buyer effort exceeds buyer motivation. Indicators include step-level dropout concentration, form abandonment patterns, checkout process exit rates, and hesitation signals at specific path positions.

Recovery Readiness dimension governs the degree to which the commercial path captures revenue from buyers who exit before completing the intended action. Recovery Readiness weakness manifests as permanent loss — visitors leave and do not return because no recovery mechanism exists to re-engage them. In isolation, Recovery Readiness weakness looks like a traffic quality problem: the conversion rate is low, and the assumption is that the audience was not qualified. The structural cause is the absence of recovery infrastructure: no re-engagement sequences, no abandoned cart recovery, no segmented re-entry pathways. The compound effect of multi-dimensional weakness is that Trust failures and Flow failures produce exits, and Recovery weakness means those exits are permanently unrecoverable.

The cross-dimensional propagation effect

The cross-dimensional propagation effect is the mechanism by which weakness in one integrity dimension corrupts the performance of adjacent dimensions. This is the core insight of dimensional weakness mapping — and the reason isolated dimension audits produce incomplete and sometimes misleading conclusions.

Trust weakness propagates into offer acceptance even when Flow Continuity architecture is structurally sound. If buyers can move through every step of the path without friction but encounter an offer stage that lacks trust infrastructure, the Flow investment is partially wasted. Buyers arrive at the conversion point prepared to act but unwilling to commit. The path is mechanically complete but structurally deficient at the trust layer. Optimizing flow further yields diminishing returns until Trust Integrity is restored.

Flow weakness propagates upstream by filtering out buyers before they reach the point where Trust infrastructure would operate. If Trust Integrity is strong at the offer stage but Flow Continuity fails at an earlier step, the buyers who would have converted on the strength of the trust architecture never arrive. The trust investment is rendered irrelevant by upstream structural failure. This is the dependency relationship that governs intervention sequencing: Flow Continuity restoration must precede Trust Integrity optimization at the offer stage because buyers must arrive before trust can operate.

Recovery weakness amplifies the revenue cost of all upstream failures. Every buyer who exits due to a Trust failure and has no recovery path represents compounded revenue loss. Every buyer who exits due to a Flow failure and encounters no re-engagement mechanism is lost twice — once to the original structural absence and once to the recovery absence. Dimensional mapping quantifies this compounding effect. The total revenue cost of a Trust Integrity failure, for example, is not simply the offer-stage dropout rate — it is that rate multiplied by the fraction of those exits that Recovery Readiness would have captured, had the recovery layer existed.

Mapping methodology

Dimensional weakness mapping follows a three-phase methodology. The first phase is the independent dimension audit: each of the three dimensions — Trust Integrity, Flow Continuity, Recovery Readiness — is assessed against its structural checklist using behavioral signal data from the path. The audit produces a weakness profile for each dimension: which specific structural elements are absent, which are present but insufficient, and which meet standard.

The second phase is the interaction matrix construction. Identified weaknesses are mapped against the three propagation pathways: Trust-to-Conversion, Flow-to-Trust, and Recovery-to-Total-Cost. This matrix identifies which weaknesses have cross-dimensional consequences and which are contained within their originating dimension. The interaction matrix governs the severity weighting applied to each identified weakness.

The third phase is priority sequencing. When multiple dimensions are weak, intervention order is not arbitrary — it is governed by the dependency relationships identified in the interaction matrix. The governing rule is upstream-first: weaknesses that prevent downstream structural elements from operating are addressed before those downstream elements are optimized. The Funnel Integrity Standard defines the benchmark for each dimension; the Protocol governs the intervention classification process; the Revenue Leak Diagnostic Engine operationalizes the methodology as a structured diagnostic workflow.

Classification and system position

Dimensional weakness mapping sits at the intersection of signal-layer analysis and intervention classification. It takes the behavioral signal data produced by diagnostic signal layers and organizes it into the three-dimension framework defined by the Funnel Integrity Standard. The output of dimensional mapping feeds directly into the Plug Ontology classification process — assigning failure classes to the identified weaknesses — and into the plug prioritization decision that governs what gets installed first.

A commercial path cannot achieve full structural integrity by correcting one dimension while leaving others unmapped. The revenue losses attributable to unmapped dimensions continue to compound regardless of improvements in the mapped dimension. Dimensional weakness mapping is the governed process that ensures no dimension is excluded from the diagnostic scope and no cross-dimensional interaction effect is missed before intervention sequencing begins.
