# SEO_POLICY.md

## 1) Purpose

SEO at Funnelplugs is not a traffic trick.
It is a trust, discoverability, and authority system.

The role of SEO is to help the asset become findable, indexable, understandable, and increasingly referential without degrading conceptual integrity.

SEO must serve the asset.
The asset must never be distorted to serve low-grade SEO.

---

## 2) Core SEO Principle

Funnelplugs will pursue **sovereign SEO**.

That means:

* no keyword stuffing,
* no thin pages,
* no mass page inflation without value,
* no manipulative internal-link clutter,
* no titles written for machines at the expense of authority,
* no metadata detached from page reality,
* no indexing of low-value pages.

SEO must strengthen:

* conceptual clarity,
* structural discoverability,
* topical authority,
* institutional trust,
* and long-term asset value.

---

## 3) SEO Objectives

The SEO system must achieve the following:

1. Ensure all high-value pages are indexable and discoverable.
2. Translate the asset’s conceptual architecture into search-friendly structure.
3. Support topical authority through disciplined internal linking.
4. Preserve quality signals across every indexed page.
5. Reflect asset maturity through structured metadata, sitemap integrity, and content coherence.

---

## 4) Indexation Rules

### 4.1 Index only pages with clear strategic value

Pages must only be indexed if they are:

* conceptually meaningful,
* structurally relevant,
* useful,
* and aligned with the reference position.

### 4.2 Do not index low-value utility states

The following should generally not be indexed unless strategically justified:

* duplicate states,
* thin filter pages,
* incomplete drafts,
* orphaned experiments,
* low-context result pages,
* debugging or temporary routes.

### 4.3 Canonical clarity is mandatory

Each indexable page must have a clear canonical target.
No ambiguity.
No conflicting duplicates.

---

## 5) Metadata Rules

### 5.1 Titles

Every indexable page must have a unique, strong, accurate title.
The title must:

* reflect page purpose,
* preserve authority,
* support discoverability,
* avoid spammy phrasing,
* and remain readable to humans first.

### 5.2 Meta descriptions

Descriptions must:

* summarize real page value,
* remain concise,
* reinforce trust,
* and avoid filler or vague marketing language.

### 5.3 Open Graph and social metadata

All key pages should have strong OG metadata aligned with the asset’s institutional quality and clarity.

---

## 6) URL and Slug Rules

### 6.1 Slugs must be clean and stable

No weak slugs.
No placeholder slugs.
No auto-generated garbage.
No unnecessary depth.

### 6.2 Slugs must reflect structure

The slug should signal role and meaning within the asset.

### 6.3 Slug validation is required

No page may pass into deployment with an empty, malformed, or strategically weak slug.

---

## 7) Internal Linking Rules

Internal linking must serve the reference architecture.

Links must:

* connect conceptually related pages,
* reinforce topical clusters,
* clarify hierarchy,
* guide authority flow,
* and reduce orphaning.

Links must not:

* exist for artificial density,
* overload the page,
* create incoherent loops,
* or function as keyword stuffing infrastructure.

---

## 8) Content Quality Rules for SEO

Every indexable page must satisfy all of the following:

* clear purpose,
* sufficient depth,
* original structure,
* coherent language,
* no padding,
* no deceptive formatting,
* and alignment with the asset’s conceptual system.

Thin content is forbidden.
Repetitive SEO filler is forbidden.
Mechanical scaling without conceptual control is forbidden.

---

## 9) Structured Data Policy

Structured data must be used carefully and truthfully.

Schema should support:

* clarity,
* search understanding,
* entity structure,
* and authority signaling.

Schema must never be abused.
No misleading markup.
No fake claim inflation.
No schema detached from real page content.

---

## 10) Search Console and Measurement Discipline

The asset should be measurable.
But analytics must not degrade UX or trust.

Search performance should be observed to improve:

* index coverage,
* crawl clarity,
* page health,
* metadata discipline,
* and authority growth.

SEO decisions must not become reactive noise.
Measurement serves strategic refinement, not panic optimization.

---

## 11) Page Classes and SEO Priority

### High Priority

* Manifesto / core thesis pages
* Protocol pages
* Standard pages
* Registry pages with real value
* high-quality reports
* deeply useful reference content

### Medium Priority

* selective supporting articles
* explanatory cluster pages
* carefully designed utility explanation pages

### Low or No Index Priority

* temporary states
* low-context results
* internal process pages
* placeholders
* partial experimental outputs

---

## 12) SEO and Monetization Relationship

Monetization must never corrupt SEO quality.

No page may be inflated purely to capture affiliate clicks.
No recommendation page may become commercially bloated.
No revenue logic may reduce conceptual seriousness.

The SEO system must preserve the asset’s dignity.

---

## 13) Enforcement Rule

No page is allowed into indexable deployment unless it meets SEO quality standards.

This should be enforced through validation logic wherever possible, including checks for:

* missing title,
* missing description,
* invalid canonical,
* weak slug,
* low page quality,
* or sitemap inconsistency.

---

## 14) Final Rule

Funnelplugs will not chase cheap search visibility.
It will build search authority through structural quality, conceptual ownership, and disciplined publication.

That is the SEO standard.
