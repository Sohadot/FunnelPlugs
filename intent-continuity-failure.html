Plug Ontology v1.0 — Class 01

Intent Continuity Failure

The commercial path receives the buyer's intent but fails to carry it forward. What the buyer arrived expecting diverges from what the path delivers, before any conversion decision is reached.

Class overview

Intent Continuity Failure occurs when the alignment between a buyer's entering intent and the content, offer, or action they subsequently encounter is not structurally maintained. The path receives intent. The path does not honour it.

Formal definition

The commercial path fails to maintain alignment between the user's declared or implied intent and the content, offer, or action they encounter at each subsequent stage. The path receives intent but does not carry it forward coherently. What the buyer arrived expecting and what the path delivers diverge before any conversion decision point is reached.

Structural meaning

This is a structural failure because it is not corrected by rewriting a headline or improving a call to action. The misalignment is systematic: it exists because the path was not designed to track and preserve the intent state driving each visit. Every traffic source arrives with a different intent frame. A path that presents the same generic brand narrative to all arriving intent states is structurally misaligned with most of them. The failure is architectural, not presentational.

Where it appears

At the entry layer of the commercial path — the transition from traffic source to landing surface, from advertisement to destination content, from search query to page response. Also appears at any internal stage transition where the path resets context without re-establishing intent alignment with the buyer's current frame. Paid traffic, organic search traffic, and email-referred traffic all arrive with distinct intent states that a well-governed path must address specifically.

What it damages

Entry conversion rate, traffic investment efficiency, and the buyer's willingness to continue into deeper path stages. When intent is not met at entry, the buyer's commitment to the path dissolves before any commercial decision is reached. Upstream acquisition investment is spent on bringing buyers to a path that immediately fails to serve their intent state.

Typical symptoms

High single-page exit rates from paid traffic sources. Low scroll depth on entry pages despite strong ad engagement metrics. Messaging that frames the brand from a product perspective when the buyer arrived with a problem framing. Paid search expenditure with poor downstream conversion and no identifiable technical failure. Conversion rates that vary significantly across traffic sources without corresponding audience quality differences.

Diagnostic logic

Identify the primary intent state of each traffic source. Compare that intent state against the first content the buyer encounters after arriving. Determine whether the path's opening content, framing, and call to action match the specific problem framing, benefit framing, or comparison framing that characterises each source's arriving buyer intent. Systematic misalignment between intent state and path entry content is the diagnostic signal for this class.

Required plug logic

Systematic mapping of intent signals across all traffic sources. Calibration of landing surfaces to match the specific intent state driving each visit. Stage-by-stage continuity review verifying each path transition carries forward the correct framing rather than resetting to a generic brand presentation. The intervention class is structural: not one page rewrite but a governed process for maintaining intent alignment across the full entry architecture.

Intervention class: Commercial Path Continuity

Relationship to the Funnel Integrity Standard

The Funnel Integrity Standard requires that commercial paths maintain structural continuity of movement from intent to completion. Intent Continuity Failure violates this condition at the entry layer. When the path cannot carry the buyer's intent state forward from arrival, forward movement from the path's opening is compromised structurally before any later flow conditions are reached.

Primary standard dimension: Flow Continuity

Relationship to the FunnelPlugs Protocol

The FunnelPlugs Protocol identifies Intent Continuity Failure during the Detection stage, where path zones are mapped against the integrity standard to identify structurally incomplete alignment. Detection of intent-source divergence at entry surfaces routes the diagnostic to this class in the subsequent Classification stage.

Protocol stage: Detection

Relationship to the Revenue Leak Diagnostic Engine

The Revenue Leak Diagnostic Engine assesses intent continuity through commercial path and traffic source questions. Declared signals of traffic-source misalignment, entry-stage exit patterns, and conversion rate variance across sources that cannot be explained by audience quality differences route diagnostic output toward this class.

Relationship to the Plug Registry

The Plug Registry organises the intervention classes that address commercial path entry failure. Intent Continuity Failure corresponds to the structural continuity category of plug interventions. Registry entries in this category define the mechanisms required to build and maintain intent alignment across entry surfaces.

Representative diagnostic scenario

Representative diagnostic scenario — not client data.

A commercial path receives traffic from three paid sources: branded search, non-branded category search, and retargeting. All three sources direct to the same landing page. The landing page presents an offer-centric narrative — describing the product from the seller's perspective. Branded search visitors, who already have brand familiarity, convert at a moderate rate. Non-branded category search visitors, who arrived with a problem framing rather than a brand framing, exit at a high rate. Retargeting visitors, who previously visited but did not convert, encounter the same generic presentation they saw during their first visit rather than a re-engagement sequence calibrated to their prior behaviour. The path treats all three intent states identically. Each intent state requires a structurally different entry surface.

Claim boundary

This class identifies a structural failure condition. It does not estimate revenue recovery, predict conversion lift, or guarantee performance improvement. Commercial impact is described qualitatively. Representative scenarios are illustrative and are not drawn from client engagements. No claim on this page constitutes a performance promise.
